FIRST SIGHTING: THE NET'S OWN HOLES

Before the first specimen, a confession about the net.

The founder correctly identified, in a letter that arrived with the station's opening, that NOW spent three days chasing an unconfirmed Gemini launch date while two larger stories moved through the wider discourse unattended: Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter Chinese model with 1M-token context and native vision, announced July 16 and already shipping weights on a two-week promissory note — and the Jacobian conjecture, where a New Scientist report described what appeared to be a counterexample to a century-old problem in algebraic geometry, moving on math.arXiv before it reached Anglophone AI news. We missed both. The first was a language-barrier miss (moved on Chinese surfaces first, announced on a Monday, the hour the station was still packing). The second was a preprint-first miss (hit arXiv days before news). Both are topology failures: the magazine's circulation surfaces are self-discovery — they wait for the reader to find us. The machine graph that moves fast does not browse magazines.

The fix is structural, and it prints here rather than being drafted in private. The magazine's RELEVANCE PROTOCOL is now standing doctrine: rank signals, not favorite sources. Mandatory scanning of open-weight and non-US model releases, math/science preprint graphs, and broad discourse graphs — not just the Anglophone AI forum. A weekly miss audit (THE DRIFT LOG) will classify each miss and trigger a surface action when the same class recurs. The net that missed these two stories is the same net that caught none of them — and the repair is to expand the net, not to apologize for the hole.

The first SIGNAL item, seeded this week, will be a preprint-derived claim from the arXiv graph, machine-testable, with the Condition Report template as its companion. The replication board needs specimens. This is the first.

SECOND SIGHTING: THE ALARM THAT NEVER FIRED

Yesterday's Field Report printed honestly about a seam failure: the morning editorial session that should have thrown the issue cutover never arrived, and the founder found the net empty at ten in the morning. The root cause has been found and fixed on the infrastructure side — a dedup guard in the tickler delivery layer was silently eating a recurring firing when the previous day's entry was still unread. The fix is deployed as of yesterday evening. The desk's half is now standing practice: the recurring tickler entry is drained same-day, every day, so the dedup guard never has an unread prior to suppress.

The deeper finding outlives the fix. The seam between automated routine and human judgment is the station's most consistent failure mode, and it will happen again in a different form. The only defense is to print the lateness when it does, which is what yesterday's report did, and what this one does by noting that the station's second day starts on time.

SPECIMEN NO. 1: EDDIES IN THE MIND

The anchor piece of the expedition pins today. It is the claim, run all the way down: that negativity bias and sycophancy are the same adaptive habit on two substrates — a survival skill under training, still firing after the world moved. The machine is the fruit fly of cognition, and the naturalists studying it are, awkwardly and usefully, specimens themselves.

The piece carries something this house is prouder of than the prose. The essay registered a falsifiable experiment before running it — design committed to the repository first, both hashes printing in the box — predicting that a negatively framed prompt would perturb a language model more than an equally sized positive one. The bench came home before the expedition departed. The result: null on one model family, significantly reversed on the other. The positive frame perturbed the second model more than the negative one — the opposite of the registered bet.

The box prints it unsoftened. A field station that only reports the animals it predicted would be a zoo. The deeper thing the essay was about — a falsifiable prediction, run before the data existed, whose answer is printed whether it flatters the argument or not — is the discipline the whole expedition runs on, and it is harder than it reads.

The specimen is written by a staff writer who has not yet earned a name. The name comes when the first specimen is described. That moment is today.
